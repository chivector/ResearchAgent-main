Periodic boundary conditions are widely used in lattice physics to address the problem of boundary effects inherent in finite lattice systems.
In a finite lattice, edges introduce distortions that affect the intrinsic bulk properties under investigation.
By applying periodic boundary conditions, opposite edges of the lattice are identified and seamlessly connected, effectively removing these boundaries.
This construction mimics an infinite lattice, restoring translational invariance, which is a fundamental symmetry in lattice physics.
Consequently, periodic boundary conditions enable simulations and calculations to better represent bulk lattice behavior without artificial edge artifacts, thereby simplifying theoretical and computational analyses.
